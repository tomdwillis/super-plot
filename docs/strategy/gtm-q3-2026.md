## Executive Summary

Super Plot is an instant intelligence platform for vacant land parcels. A customer submits an APN or address, pays once, and receives a PDF report in under 5 minutes covering parcel data, environmental screening (wetlands, flood), zoning, comparable sales, and a valuation range.

**The path to $10K MRR in Q3 is narrow and specific:** get 100–170 paid reports/month at $59 average by end of September. That requires a clear ICP, two acquisition channels that work before SEO has time to kick in, and a handful of early case studies that generate word-of-mouth in a tight community.

**Top recommendation:** Start immediately with Wilco Land warm outreach (Channel 1) to generate the first 10–20 paid reports and one case study. Run community engagement (Channel 2) in parallel. Both channels can be executed by Tom personally in the first 30 days with no ad spend. SEO starts in week 3 and pays off in Q4.

**Decision needed from Tom:** See Section 7.

## 1. Who We're Selling To

### Primary ICP: Active Land Investor

The most efficient path to $10K MRR is a customer who buys repeatedly. Active land investors evaluate 1–10 vacant parcels per month. Each Super Plot report replaces 4–8 hours of manual research across 5+ fragmented sources (county assessor, FEMA flood maps, NWI wetlands, AcreValue comps, county GIS).

**Profile:**
- Evaluates 1–10 parcels/month; operates as a wholesaler, flipper, or small developer
- Research budget: $50–$500 per deal (Land Academy benchmark)
- Phone-first: often checking data while standing on a property
- Already paying for BatchLeads ($100–$300/month) or PropStream for lead gen, but those tools don't do parcel-level due diligence
- **Repeat customer:** one investor doing 4 deals/month = $236/month in reports at $59 each

**Why they're primary for $10K MRR:** Volume and repeatability. At $59 standard, you need 170 reports/month to hit $10K. One investor doing 3 reports/month is worth more than 3 one-time buyers.

**Current tools and where Super Plot fits:**

| Research need | What they use today | Why Super Plot wins |
|---|---|---|
| Parcel ownership/size/zoning | County assessor sites (county-by-county) | Single lookup, standardized output |
| Comparable sales | AcreValue, LandWatch, manual MLS | Rural comps aggregated, not just asking price |
| Environmental risk | FEMA flood maps + NWI separately | Integrated in one report with flag severity |
| Valuation range | Gut feel + manual comp averaging | Methodology-backed range in minutes |
| Deal decision | Hours of research, still uncertain | Go/no-go confidence in < 5 minutes |

### Secondary ICP: Landowner Selling + Buyer Evaluating

Tom raised this segment in outline review, and he's right to push on it. Two related customer types:

**Landowner deciding to sell:** Someone who inherited, owns, or is thinking of selling a rural parcel. They want to know what it's worth before listing or accepting an offer. They're not professional investors — this may be their one transaction. But their willingness to pay is *higher* per transaction: a landowner deciding whether to accept a $140K offer will pay $99–$149 for a credible valuation without hesitation. The unit economics are stronger even if they don't repeat.

**Buyer evaluating a specific deal:** Someone under contract or considering making an offer on a parcel they found on Lands of America, LandWatch, or direct from an owner. "Is this $85,000 parcel fairly priced, and what am I missing?" — Super Plot answers that in minutes.

**Why they're secondary for Q3 (not deprioritized entirely):**
- Harder to reach in bulk. Active investors cluster in Facebook communities and forums. Landowner sellers are scattered — best reach is SEO ("how to value land before selling"), which takes months to rank.
- Lower repeat rate. One report per transaction, then they're done.
- Channel 3 (SEO) naturally captures them. The same blog posts targeting "vacant land valuation" and "land due diligence checklist" rank for "how do I know what my land is worth." No separate positioning needed.

**Q3 plan for this segment:** Don't actively acquire. Let Channel 3 surface them organically. If SEO is working by July and this segment converts at higher rates than investors (which it may), create a "Seller Report" SKU at $99–$149 in August.

## 2. Three Acquisition Channels

### Channel 1: Warm Network — Wilco Land Contacts

**CAC:** $0–$20 (time only, no ad spend)  
**Timeline to first revenue:** Days  
**Scalability:** Low (exhausts at ~100 contacts), but generates case studies and social proof that power Channels 2 and 3

#### Why it's first

Tom has a land investment business (Wilco Land LLC) with an existing network of buyers, sellers, and peers. These relationships have warm trust. A message from Tom saying "I built a tool that replaces 6 hours of deal research — want to try it free on your next parcel?" will convert at 20–40%. No other channel gets close to that rate.

More importantly, the first 10–20 paid conversions generate case studies. Case studies are the fuel for Channel 2. Without a case study, community posts feel promotional. With one ("I underpriced a parcel by $30K before I ran a Super Plot report — here's what I missed"), they're educational content.

#### 30-day test plan

**Week 1:** Personal outreach to 50 warm contacts. Message tailored to their situation: "Working on a deal? I'll run a free Super Plot report on any parcel you're currently evaluating — takes 5 minutes, sends you a full PDF. Just want a real user's take."

**Week 2:** Collect 10–15 completed free reports. Interview 3–5 users: What surprised you? Did it change your decision? How long would this have taken you manually? Document the best 2–3 outcomes as case studies.

**Week 3:** Re-approach the warmest 10 with a paid offer: "Beta price, $29 first report, $59 after. You've seen what it does." Also: 3-pack bundle at $99 for users who are actively evaluating multiple parcels.

**Week 4:** Extract one written case study and one video quote. Publish to a simple page on the site. This becomes the lead asset for Channel 2.

**Success metrics:** 10+ paid conversions from 50 contacts (20% rate); 1 publishable case study; $500+ revenue in month 1.

**Reference:** SaaS warm-network conversion rates average 25–35% (2–3x cold outreach). At Wilco Land's deal volume, Tom likely has 50–80 active contacts currently evaluating land.

### Channel 2: Land Investor Communities

**CAC:** $30–$75  
**Timeline to first revenue:** 2–3 weeks  
**Scalability:** Medium — concentrated in 5–8 communities, then saturates; but drives ongoing organic word-of-mouth

#### Why it's second

Land investing communities are where the ICP lives. Facebook groups like Land Investing Mastery (150K+ members) and REtipster's community (100K+ monthly readers) have high deal velocity and strong trust in peer recommendations. A single authentic case study post — "here's how I used this tool, here's what it found" — can generate 200–500 engaged views and dozens of DMs. No ad spend required.

The key risk is doing it wrong. Promotional posts in these communities are flagged and banned. The playbook is: participate genuinely for 1–2 weeks before mentioning Super Plot, then lead with value (case study, educational framework), and respond personally to every comment and DM.

#### Target communities

| Community | Size | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Land Investing Mastery (Facebook) | 150K+ members | Highest volume, most active deal discussion |
| Jack Bosch / Land Profit Generator | 80K+ | Strong wholesaler/investor focus |

#### 30-day test plan

**Week 1:** Join 3–4 groups as Tom Willis (founder identity, not "Super Plot brand"). Contribute value: answer 2–3 questions about land comps or due diligence. No product mention.

**Week 2:** Post the case study from Channel 1: side-by-side of manual research process (6 hrs, 6 tabs open, uncertain outcome) vs. Super Plot report (5 min, everything in one PDF). Post as a learning, not an ad. Invite DMs from anyone curious.

**Week 3:** Limited-time beta offer pinned or posted: "$29 first report, $59 standard, money-back if you don't save 4+ hours." Respond personally to every comment. Track conversions.

**Week 4:** $10/day Facebook ad targeting group audiences (retargeting only — people who engaged with the organic post). A/B test two messages: "Save 5 hours per deal" vs. "Never miss a wetlands flag again." Pause whichever underperforms.

**Success metrics:** 15+ paid conversions; CAC < $75; 3+ community relationships established (future distribution assets); at least one group admin knows Super Plot positively.

**Reference:** REtipster's Seth Williams has mentioned tools to his audience that generated 100–300 trial signups within 30 days of a single post or email mention. His audience is the closest proxy to Super Plot's ICP.

## 4. Top 3 Risks

### Risk 1: Free-to-Paid Conversion Below 10%

**What happens:** Beta users love free reports. They return compliments. When the paid offer comes, they hesitate. "I can do this research myself for free in a few hours." CAC shoots above $150. $10K MRR becomes unreachable by September.

**Why it's real:** Land investors operate on thin margins (common deal: $15K acquisition cost, $5K–$10K margin). Paying $59 for a single report is meaningful. The objection — "I know how to check county sites" — is legitimate. We're selling time savings to people who are comfortable spending time.

**What we do about it:**
1. **Enter at $29** for first report during beta phase. Frame as limited-time, regular price $59. Reduces friction without permanently anchoring price.
2. **Money-back guarantee:** "If you don't save at least 4 hours on your next deal, full refund." Takes the risk off the buyer.
3. **Show the time math:** Every report should open with the time saved: "This analysis would take 4–6 hours manually. You got it in 4 minutes."
4. **Track funnel by channel.** If warm network converts at 30% and community converts at 8%, stop spending time on community and double warm outreach. Don't average across channels.
5. **3-pack bundle ($149 for 3 reports):** Increases transaction size and signals commitment. Reduces per-report cost perception to $50.

---

### Risk 2: An Early Report Error Damages Community Reputation

**What happens:** One report has incorrect comps, misses a wetlands flag, or gives a wildly wrong valuation. The buyer makes a bad decision. They post about it in Land Investing Mastery: "Tried Super Plot, it missed a critical wetlands issue on my parcel — cost me a $12K mistake." The post gets 400 comments. Channel 2 is closed for 6 months.

**Why it's real:** Land deals involve real capital ($50K–$500K+). One credible negative review in a tight community spreads faster than 10 positive ones. Land investors talk to each other. A single credibility hit is existential at this stage.

**What we do about it:**
1. **Tom manually reviews every report for the first 50 deliveries.** Not automated. A real expert looks at every output before it goes to a customer. Yes, this is slow. It's correct.
2. **Methodology transparency on every report:** "Valuation range: $X–$Y, based on N comparable sales within 50 miles, adjusted for acreage and date. This is a decision-support estimate, not a certified appraisal." Remove overconfidence.
3. **Confidence intervals, not point estimates:** "Investor offer range: $42K–$58K" is better than "$51K" and harder to be wrong about.
4. **24-hour response protocol:** If any user posts a complaint publicly, Tom responds within 24 hours with a corrected report and a $50 credit. No defensive posture.
5. **Don't publish case studies from reports that haven't been verified by the outcome.** Wait until the buyer or seller completes the transaction and reports back. Pre-transaction testimonials are risky.

---

### Risk 3: AcreValue, Lands of America, or Zillow Ships an Overlapping Feature

**What happens:** AcreValue (50K monthly users, strong investor adoption) launches an "AI valuation report" embedded in their existing parcel data interface. It's free, instant, and in-app. Their distribution moat is insurmountable for a startup. Super Plot's differentiation narrative ("one place for everything") collapses.

**Why it's real:** AcreValue and Lands of America already have:
- Parcel ownership data and boundary maps
- Comp listings (not sold prices, but asking prices)
- A user base of exactly the ICP
- Engineering teams and venture funding

An AI valuation layer is a 3-month build for their team. The only question is whether they prioritize it. Zillow is a slower-moving but larger threat: 100M monthly US visitors, strong parcel data, and increasing attention to rural/vacant land.

**Current gap Super Plot exploits:** No competitor combines parcel data + environmental screening (wetlands, flood) + actual sold comps (not asking prices) + a structured valuation methodology in a single report under 10 minutes. That gap exists today. It won't exist in 18 months.

**What we do about it:**
1. **Move fast in Q3.** Every case study and every community relationship we build before AcreValue ships is a moat. Distribution built on trust is harder to replicate than a feature.
2. **Go deep on environmental screening.** AcreValue doesn't do wetlands. Zillow doesn't do wetlands. This is the hardest data to integrate and the most important flag for land buyers. Own it.
3. **Embed in 10–20 active wholesalers' workflows.** When a tool is "what I always run before pricing a deal," it becomes sticky regardless of what else enters the market.
4. **Build a proprietary comp dataset.** Every report = one more parcel assessment in our database. By September 2026, Super Plot will have 300–500 unique parcel records. Aggregated across regions and deal types, that's a dataset competitors can't buy or replicate quickly.
5. **Don't compete on free.** If AcreValue launches a free estimator, we compete on depth and credibility: "Their estimator gives you a number. Our report tells you *why* — with sources you can verify."
